# Padel House Digital Experience: Demo Script

Use this guide in a client meeting to present the demo clearly, confidently, and without overpromising.

## Before you start (30 seconds)

- Open `index.html` in Spanish unless the client prefers English.
- Say clearly: **"This is a commercial demo with sample data. It is not a finished system."**
- Frame the goal: **"We built this to help you see what daily operations could feel like, and to discover what you would value most before investing in a real platform."**

## 5-minute walkthrough

### 1. Executive Dashboard (60 seconds)

**Open:** `index.html`

**Say:**
- "This is a sample operations view for Padel House across four stores."
- "In the first screen you see revenue, members, inventory turnover, and average ticket, all with example data."
- "The hero explains that this demo helps validate priorities before building anything real."

**Show:**
- Hero banner and **Comenzar el recorrido** CTA
- One chart and one insight card
- **Siguiente paso** card at the bottom

**Transition:** "Let's follow the journey the demo suggests."

### 2. Customer Insights (45 seconds)

**Open:** `customer-insights.html` (via next-step card or sidebar)

**Say:**
- "Here the question is: which customers deserve attention?"
- "Segments, retention, and top customers are shown as business signals, not as a CRM database."

**Show:**
- KPI row
- One chart
- One action card: invite inactive buyers back

### 3. Inventory Intelligence (45 seconds)

**Open:** `inventory-intelligence.html`

**Say:**
- "Which products need action?"
- "Low stock alerts and coverage help the owner decide where to reorder or transfer stock."

**Show:**
- Alerts table or warehouse view
- Action card: reorder before demand peaks

### 4. Product Hub (45 seconds)

**Open:** `product-hub.html`

**Say:**
- "Products are shown as business assets, not as database rows."
- "Featured items, stock health, and bundles show how a premium catalog could look."

**Show:**
- Health strip
- Category filter
- **Simular una venta** CTA

### 5. Experience Center (75 seconds)

**Open:** `experience-center.html`

**Say:**
- "This simulates the selling experience. Nothing is saved."
- "Select a Padel Club member, add products, apply a discount, and preview a digital receipt."

**Do live:**
1. Select **María R.** (Platinum)
2. Add a racket and an accessory
3. Tap a suggested add-on
4. Choose a payment method
5. Open **Ver recibo digital**

**Important line:**
- "The receipt is a demo preview only. It has no fiscal value and is not a real POS or invoicing system."

### 6. Growth Opportunities (30 seconds, optional if time)

**Open:** `growth-opportunities.html`

**Say:**
- "This closes the conversation with potential paths and a staged roadmap."
- "Nothing here is a commitment. It is a way to discuss priorities."

**Show:**
- Potential paths cards
- Stage 1: Validate now

## Key talking points

- Built for **Padel House**, with Dominican Republic retail context (RD$, store names, padel categories).
- Helps the owner **validate priorities** before investing in software.
- Shows **business value**, not internal admin screens.
- Bilingual: Spanish default, English optional.
- Fully static: safe to share, easy to host on GitHub Pages.
- The Experience Center is an **immersive preview**, not operational checkout software.

## Questions to ask the client

1. "Which screen would help your team most in the first 10 seconds of the day?"
2. "Would you rather start with inventory control, customer retention, or the in-store selling experience?"
3. "Which KPIs on the executive dashboard match how you actually measure the business today?"
4. "Does the Product Hub reflect how you think about your catalog, or what is missing?"
5. "If we built only two things first, which two would create the most value for Padel House?"

## What not to promise

Do **not** say or imply:

- That this is a finished ERP, CRM, or accounting platform
- That receipts, taxes, or payments are legally valid
- That data syncs with real stores, banks, or suppliers
- That login, roles, permissions, or multi-user workflows exist
- That inventory, sales, or customer records can be edited and saved
- That the demo numbers are real business results
- That a full platform can be delivered as-is from this demo

## Recommended closing question

**"After seeing this walkthrough, which part of Padel House's daily operation would you want to improve first, and why?"**

That answer becomes the starting point for scoping a real project.

## Suggested follow-up

- Note the client's top 2 priorities from the session.
- Share the GitHub Pages link for async review.
- Offer a second session focused only on their chosen modules.
